Conference Material

Position Paper

To maintain a high-quality conference, a position paper is mandatory for award consideration in KYOMUN. Delegates must submit a position paper by August 7th after their country allocation on July 31st. However, it is highly recommended to start researching the agenda before country allocation. The position paper is a 1-2-page report that delegates must write regarding their country's position on the agenda. The position paper guide can be accessed through the link below. AI-written position papers are banned and their submission will substantially affect the delegate's ability to earn awards.

Resolution Guide

The resolution is the product of the MUN conference that lists solutions proposed by the delegates. Delegates are not allowed to write resolutions before the conference.

Chair Report

The Chair Report is an introductory background guide for delegates regarding the committee and agenda. Delegates must read through the Chair Report to gain a broad understanding of their objectives and roles. The conference strongly encourages delegates to research the agenda beyond the Chair Report.
